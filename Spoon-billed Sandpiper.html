Spoon-billed Sandpiper

The Spoon-billed Sandpiper (Calidris pygmaea) is a critically endangered bird species that breeds in northeastern Russia and migrates to Southeast Asia during the winter. It is known for its distinctive spoon-shaped bill, which it uses to forage for small invertebrates in mudflats and shallow waters.

Spoon-billed Sandpipers face numerous threats, including habitat loss, degradation of stopover sites, and hunting along their migratory routes. Conservation efforts are underway to protect their breeding and wintering habitats and to raise awareness about the importance of preserving this unique species.
