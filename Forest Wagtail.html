Forest Wagtail

The Forest Wagtail (Dendronanthus indicus) is a small passerine bird in the family Motacillidae. It is found in forests and woodland areas across Asia, where it can be seen foraging on the ground for insects and small invertebrates.

Forest Wagtails are known for their distinctive behavior of wagging their tails up and down while foraging, which helps flush out insects from the ground. They have a plain olive-brown plumage with white underparts and a long tail.
