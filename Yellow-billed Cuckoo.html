Yellow-billed Cuckoo

The Yellow-billed Cuckoo (Coccyzus americanus) is a medium-sized cuckoo native to North and Central America. It is recognized by its long tail, slender body, and distinctive yellow bill.

Yellow-billed Cuckoos inhabit riparian woodlands and forests, where they feed on insects, particularly caterpillars. They are known for their secretive behavior and often remain hidden within dense foliage.
