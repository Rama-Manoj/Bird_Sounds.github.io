Black-capped Vireo

The Black-capped Vireo (Vireo atricapilla) is a small songbird native to the United States and Mexico. It is distinguished by its black cap, white spectacles, and yellowish underparts.

Black-capped Vireos inhabit scrubby areas and open woodlands, where they forage for insects and spiders. They are known for their melodious songs, which are often heard during the breeding season.
