South Island Saddleback

The South Island Saddleback, also known as the South Island Tieke, is a forest bird endemic to New Zealand. It is characterized by its striking black and chestnut plumage and distinctive saddle-shaped white patch on its back.

Once considered extinct on the mainland, conservation efforts have led to successful reintroductions in predator-controlled areas. The saddleback plays a vital role in forest ecosystems by helping control insect populations and dispersing seeds.
