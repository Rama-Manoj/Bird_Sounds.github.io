Kirtland's Warbler

Kirtland's Warbler (Setophaga kirtlandii) is a small songbird that breeds in young jack pine forests of Michigan, Wisconsin, and Ontario, Canada. It is named after Jared P. Kirtland, an Ohio doctor and amateur naturalist.

This bird is one of the rarest warblers in North America and is listed as an endangered species. Conservation efforts include habitat management and control of the brown-headed cowbird, a nest parasite that negatively affects Kirtland's Warbler populations.
