Yellow-eyed Penguin

The Yellow-eyed Penguin, also known as Hoiho, is a penguin species native to New Zealand. It is one of the rarest and most endangered penguin species in the world.

Characterized by its distinctive yellow eyes and yellow band across its head, the Yellow-eyed Penguin primarily feeds on fish and squid. Loss of habitat, predation, and human disturbance are significant threats to its survival.
