Mottled Petrel

The Mottled Petrel is a medium-sized seabird found in the Pacific Ocean. It is known for its mottled gray and white plumage and long, slender wings adapted for dynamic soaring.

These petrels nest in burrows on remote islands and spend most of their lives at sea. They are threatened by introduced predators and habitat disturbance, with conservation efforts focusing on protecting nesting sites and eradicating invasive species.
