Marbled Murrelet

The Marbled Murrelet (Brachyramphus marmoratus) is a small seabird that breeds in the coastal forests of the Pacific Northwest. It is known for its distinctive marbled plumage and short wings.

Marbled Murrelets nest high in the branches of old-growth trees, often far inland from the coast. They feed primarily on small fish and marine invertebrates. Due to habitat loss and degradation, the Marbled Murrelet is considered a threatened species.
