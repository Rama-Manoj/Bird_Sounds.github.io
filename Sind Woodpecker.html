Sind Woodpecker

The Sind Woodpecker (Dendrocopos assimilis) is a species of woodpecker found in the arid regions of the Indian subcontinent. It is characterized by its black and white plumage with distinctive red markings on the head and neck.

Sind Woodpeckers inhabit scrubland, agricultural areas, and semi-desert regions where they forage for insects by pecking into tree bark. They are known for their drumming sounds, which they use to communicate and establish territories.
