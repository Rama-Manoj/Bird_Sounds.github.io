White-rumped Vulture

The White-rumped Vulture (Gyps bengalensis) is a critically endangered vulture species found in South Asia. It is characterized by its white rump, brownish body, and large wingspan. White-rumped Vultures play a crucial role in the ecosystem by scavenging carcasses and preventing the spread of diseases.

Population of White-rumped Vultures has declined rapidly due to factors such as habitat loss, poisoning from veterinary drugs, and reduction in food availability. Conservation efforts, including vulture-safe feeding sites and breeding programs, are underway to stabilize and increase their population.
