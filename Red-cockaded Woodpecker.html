Red-cockaded Woodpecker

The Red-cockaded Woodpecker (Leuconotopicus borealis) is a species of woodpecker found in southeastern pine forests of the United States. It is known for its distinctive black-and-white plumage and small size.

Red-cockaded Woodpeckers are cavity-nesting birds, and they excavate cavities in living pine trees. They primarily feed on insects found under the bark of pine trees. Due to habitat loss and fragmentation, the Red-cockaded Woodpecker is considered an endangered species.
