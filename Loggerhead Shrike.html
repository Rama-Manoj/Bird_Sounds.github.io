Loggerhead Shrike

The Loggerhead Shrike (Lanius ludovicianus) is a passerine bird known for its habit of impaling prey on thorns or barbed wire. It has a grayish-white underbelly, black wings with white patches, and a distinctive black mask.

Loggerhead Shrikes primarily feed on insects, small mammals, and occasionally small birds. Despite their predatory behavior, they are considered songbirds. Loss of habitat and pesticide use have contributed to their decline in some areas.
