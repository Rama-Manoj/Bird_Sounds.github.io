Indian Pitta

The Indian Pitta (Pitta brachyura) is a small, colorful bird found in the Indian subcontinent. It is known for its distinctive bright plumage with shades of green, blue, yellow, and red. Indian Pittas are shy birds that inhabit dense forests and scrublands.

They are primarily insectivorous, feeding on a variety of insects and their larvae. Indian Pittas have a melodious and repetitive call, often heard during the breeding season. Their population is declining due to habitat loss and degradation.
