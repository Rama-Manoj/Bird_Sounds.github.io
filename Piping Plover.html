Piping Plover

The Piping Plover (Charadrius melodus) is a small shorebird found along the coastlines and inland beaches of North America. It is recognized for its pale plumage, distinctive black neckband, and orange legs.

Piping Plovers nest in shallow depressions in the sand and feed on small invertebrates found in beach and shoreline habitats. They face threats from habitat loss, disturbance, and predation, and are classified as a threatened species.
