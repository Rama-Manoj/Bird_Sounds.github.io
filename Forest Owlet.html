Forest Owlet

The Forest Owlet (Heteroglaux blewitti) is a critically endangered owl species endemic to the forests of central India. It was once thought to be extinct until its rediscovery in 1997. The Forest Owlet is characterized by its distinctive facial disk, large yellow eyes, and brownish plumage with white spots and streaks.

Forest Owlets inhabit dense deciduous forests where they hunt small mammals, birds, and insects. Due to habitat loss and degradation, as well as hunting and trapping, the Forest Owlet population remains extremely low, and conservation efforts are underway to protect its remaining habitat and ensure its survival.
