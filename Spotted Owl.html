Spotted Owl

The Spotted Owl (Strix occidentalis) is a species of true owl native to North America. It is primarily a forest-dwelling bird, found in old-growth forests and mixed coniferous forests.

Spotted Owls are known for their distinctive spotted plumage and large, dark eyes. They are nocturnal hunters, preying on small mammals such as mice and voles. Due to habitat loss and other factors, Spotted Owl populations have declined in recent years.
