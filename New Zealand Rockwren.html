New Zealand Rockwren

The New Zealand Rockwren, also known as the South Island Rockwren, is a small, insectivorous bird endemic to New Zealand. It is well adapted to life in alpine and subalpine environments.

Rockwrens are known for their distinctive habit of hopping and climbing among rock piles and boulder fields in search of insects. They have short wings and a distinctive short tail. Due to habitat loss and introduced predators, they are considered vulnerable.
