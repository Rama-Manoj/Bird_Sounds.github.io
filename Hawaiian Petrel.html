Hawaiian Petrel

The Hawaiian Petrel (Pterodroma sandwichensis), also known as 'Ua'u, is a seabird endemic to Hawaii. It has a dark plumage, white underparts, and a hooked bill.

Hawaiian Petrels nest in burrows on remote, mountainous islands and feed at sea. They face threats from habitat destruction, invasive species, and light pollution. Conservation efforts aim to protect nesting sites and reduce threats.
