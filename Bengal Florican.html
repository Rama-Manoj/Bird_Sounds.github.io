Bengal Florican

The Bengal Florican (Houbaropsis bengalensis) is a critically endangered bird species found in the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its long neck, long legs, and distinctive plumage, with males displaying black plumage with white patches and a large inflatable throat sac during the breeding season.

Bengal Floricans inhabit tall grasslands and wetlands, where they forage for insects and small invertebrates. They are known for their elaborate courtship displays, which involve leaping and fluttering flights by the males to attract females.
